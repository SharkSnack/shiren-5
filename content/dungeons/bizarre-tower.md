Dungeon where you progress through 15 floor ranges using special rules in the order of your choice.<br/>
It's balanced so that the quality of items generally increases the further you progress.

The dungeon has both day and night, but time of day doesn't change based on elapsed turns.<br/>
Field of view isn't restricted during the day except for Monster House floor ranges,<br/>
and Monster Houses never get generated naturally outside of Monster House floor ranges.

### General

There are 15 stairs representing floor ranges at the start, and you're free to challenge them in any order.<br/>
Each floor range can only be chosen once, and you must go through all 15 to clear the dungeon.

Mixers appear on 8-10F, so plan a route that lets you obtain items for synthesis before that floor range.

Synthesis Pots are somewhat common, so if you're not struggling with synthesis, save the Mixer stairs<br/>for the 2nd half to get past floor ranges where tricky monsters spawn. (Ex: Ornery Tanks between 33-35F)<br/>The majority of spawns turn into Mixer monsters, which tend to be easier to deal with.

The Mixer floor range has a unique monster table.<br/>(30F+ is Mixermon, Mixergon, Kappa Pest, Green Zalokleft, and Concusschin)

Food, staves, and talismans can only be found on the ground within stairs 9-15,<br/>
so be sure to check your remaining staff uses and food prior to choosing a new floor range.<br/>
Gitan can only be found on the ground within stairs 8-12, so it's nice to visit 8-11 before 12.<br/>
However, stairs 9-10 are dangerous zones, so don't overextend for the sake of Gitan.

Special Monster Houses are generated pretty often in Bizarre Tower.<br/>
Consider ignoring Monster Houses if you didn't start in one / if the stairs isn't located in one.<br/>
Doing so lowers risk, and lets you check other rooms without encountering many enemies.

Visit stairs 1-2 after you've obtained Synthesis Pots or when it overlaps with natural Mixer spawns,<br/>
since you'll be able to synthesize the weapons and shields you find right away.

### Rules

#### Only weapons and shields on the ground (1 floor, 3 floors)

Popular choice to start with since you're guaranteed to find equipment.<br/>
Status weapons can't be found on the ground, so upgrade value is the focus if chosen before Mixers.

#### Only grass on the ground (3 floors)

Easy rule since grasses are identified in this dungeon, but don't choose it when Grass Kids appear.<br/>
Save Perception Grass for "HP doesn't regenerate, and lots of traps." floors.<br/>
Not a bad choice to overlap it with the first Mixer zone (8-10F).

#### Only scrolls on the ground (1 floor, 3 floors)

Read unidentified scrolls while standing on the stairs in case it's a bad scroll like Swift Foe or Pot Dog.<br/>
It's a bit uncommon to find 2 copies of a scroll due to the large variety of scrolls that can be found,<br/>
so try to identify them without using them if possible.

#### Only pots on the ground (1 floor, 3 floors)

Preservation Pots to expand inventory, Identify Pots to identify items, Fever Pots to duplicate items, etc.<br/>
Some choose this first to start with Preservation Pots instead of waiting until their inventory is full.<br/>
Zalokleft Pots can be found, so consider visiting this zone before the "Stores appear." floors.

#### Only Gitan on the ground (4 floors)

Usually chosen before the "Stores appear." floors.<br/>
Gitan bags are generally around 400-500G, so you can't use them to one-shot late game monsters.

#### HP doesn't regenerate, and lots of traps (2 floors, 4 floors)

Perception Grass and Trap Deletion Scrl make the "lots of traps" portion a non-issue.<br/>
It's best to do these floors mid to late game, since HP regeneration slows down with higher max HP.<br/>
However, some players prefer to clear them early while enemies aren't very threatening.<br/>
This is one of the few locations where Trap Del. Staff has a chance to shine.

#### It's always night (3 floors)

Basically a bonus zone if you have good abilities, but there are some dangerous floors like 41-43F.<br/>
Some like to get these floors over with in the first half so that they don't need to carry torches.

#### Stores appear (2 floors)

Nice for the first Mixer zone (8-10F), since price identification lets you figure out synthesis ingredients.<br/>
Also a solid choice when you've collected lots of unidentified items and lack items to identify them.<br/>
Don't choose this zone if dangerous monsters appear, since you might not get a chance to shop.<br/>
If you're unlucky, the generated shops could be elite shops, meaning you won't be able to enter.

#### Mostly Mixer family monsters spawn (5 floors)

Can be used to avoid dangerous floor ranges by turning most enemies into Mixer monsters.<br/>
Be warned that higher level Mixers have high attack, so don't get careless trying to synthesize.

#### Monster Houses appear (3 floors, 7 floors)

A Special Monster House at the start often ends the run, but waiting until the end is also risky.<br/>
One option is to equip a torch and check what monsters are present before entering the room.<br/>
Some choose 7 floors of Monster Houses right away, and then synthesize using Mixers afterward.<br/>
Others who like a thrill can choose these stairs last to enjoy 10 floors of late game Monster Houses.<br/>
In any case, avoid Tiger Tosser and Pumphantasm floors if challenging these stairs early on.

### Equipment

#### Weapon

Status weapons can only be found in shops, so they aren't likely to be a main weapon option.<br/>
Weapons with 2 bracelet resonance are good, since many useful bracelets can be found on the ground.<br/>
Bladite and Red Blade are shop-exclusive, so you'll often end up with Dotanuki, Beast Fang, or Katana.<br/>
Water Cutter or Scythe are also solid choices, but other type effective weapons are a bit lackluster.

#### Weapon Runes

Prioritize status inflicting runes. (<span class="greenText">Paralyzing</span>, <span class="greenText">Sedating</span>, <span class="greenText">Bored</span>, <span class="greenText">Confusing</span>, etc.)<br/> <span class="greenText">Anti-Aquatic</span> and <span class="greenText">Anti-Floating</span> are the best type effective runes, and <span class="greenText">Anti-Metal</span> is nice for Ornery Tank.<br/>
Have a Karakuroid (6-8F) create an Iron Arrow Trap to obtain an Iron Arrow for synthesis purposes.

#### Shield

Targite and Red Shield are shop-exclusive, so you'll often end up with Wolfshead, Beast Shield, or Iron Targe.<br/>
Binary Shield makes for an effective main shield, provided you don't care about 2 bracelet resonance.<br/>
If you visit stairs 12 (Stores appear) near the beginning and find a Day Shield, don't hesitate to use it.

#### Shield Runes

You can't go wrong with <span class="greenText">Anti-Blast</span>, <span class="greenText">Bit</span>, <span class="greenText">Agile</span>, and <span class="greenText">Anti-Theft</span>.<br/>
This dungeon only has a depth of 45 floors, so others like <span class="greenText">Anti-Fire</span>, <span class="greenText">Anti-Peck</span>, and <span class="greenText">Rustproof</span><br/>
aren't essential because higher level Dragons, Gyadons, and Mudkins don't spawn.

#### Bracelets

Monster Detector and Item Detector can be found on the ground, so pick up every unidentified bracelet.<br/>
If you start with Monster House floors, use Strip, Spin, and Sleep traps to test for bracelet effects.<br/>
You can also visit stairs 12 (Stores appear) after you've collected some bracelets and identify by price,<br/>
or if you understand the risks of equipping a cursed bracelet, go ahead and equip them on the spot.

### Other Items

#### Projectiles

Collect arrows using traps found in Monster Houses or created by Karakuroids (6-8F).<br/>
Poison Arrows help against late game monsters, and you'll want 1 Iron Arrow to synthesize <span class="greenText">Anti-Metal</span>.

#### Scrolls

Chances to check prices are limited, so either read scrolls or use an Identify Scroll or Identify Pot.<br/>
Scrolls with negative effects are common, so it's best to read scrolls while standing on the exit.<br/>Reading a Pot Dog Scroll near the beginning of the adventure can end the run.

#### Staves

Swap and Pinning staves are effective on floors that don't have walls.<br/>
These two are nice for Monster House floor ranges in particular, since map generation can change.

#### Pots

Preservation, Heal, and fragrance pots are the essentials.<br/>
Fever Pots can be found on the ground, making them more common than in other dungeons.

#### Talismans

Throwing a talisman carries some risk, but they're nice to have because scrolls are hard to identify.<br/>
Sleep and Fear talismans can be used to effectively shut down most monsters.

#### Grass

It's good to carry some HP restoring grasses until you obtain Revival Grass, Swift Grass, and Heal Pot.<br/>
There's little need for other grasses, but Perception Grass is nice for floors where HP doesn't regenerate.

### Floor Guide

#### Early Game

Choose Monster House floors early, since HP regenerates quickly and dangerous monsters don't appear.<br/>
You'll also find a variety of items in time for the first Mixer zone (8-10F), helping with consistency.<br/>
However, Special Monster Houses can appear, so expect to restart a few times before things go smoothly.<br/>
Some players like to wait until they're able to defeat Mixers before attempting Monster House floors.

Monster House floors on 6-10F don't have walls, making it hard to collect arrows using Karakuroids (6-8F).<br/>
Therefore, some postpone Monster House floors until after 8F so that they can collect more arrows.

Choose floors where HP doesn't regenerate around the time your HP regeneration slows down.<br/>
Check for traps and take things one step at a time, ideally while keeping Super status active.<br/>
Do these after stairs 3 (only grass) so you have Perception and HP restoring grasses on hand.

That said, it's not necessarily a bad idea to tackle stairs 9 and 10 right away.<br/>
You should be able to defeat most enemies in 1 or 2 hits, and you'll hardly take damage with a shield.<br/>
Monsters hit somewhat hard starting from 3F, so aim for a shield with at least 10 defense if possible.

Wood Arrow Traps appear between 1-5F, so walk along walls and check for traps to collect arrows.<br/>
Arrow count and equipment strength comes down to luck when attempting stairs 10 early,<br/>
so if you don't find what you need pretty quickly, you'll probably end up collapsed.

#### Mid Game

Avoid 3 (grass), 6-7 (pots), and 14-15 (Monster Houses) on Digestiphant and Kappa Pest floors (29-31F).<br/>
Kappa Pest makes it hard to obtain items, so choose stairs that don't include valuable item categories.<br/>
※ Stairs 8 (only Gitan) is safe on Kappa Pest floors, since Lv2 Bored Kappas don't throw Gitan.

#### Late Game

Stairs 13 (Mixers) can spawn powerful Mixergons from 30F, so it's not worth the risk to synthesize.<br/>
If you're ready to rush stairs, choose stairs 13 when it overlaps with dangerous enemy floors.<br/>
Examples: Ornery Tank (33-35F), MC Wizard (37-38F).

#### Other Notes

6-8F: Karakuroids appear, so collect arrows --- 1 Iron Arrow for <span class="greenText">Anti-Metal</span>, at least.

6-10F: Maps on these floors don't have walls if you choose stairs 14 or 15 (Monster Houses).<br/>
While it's easier to find items for synthesis on MH floors, it's harder to collect arrows using traps.<br/>Field of view is also limited on MH floors, so you risk getting items cursed by Curse Girls in hallways,<br/>
and Fearabbits can end a run if you don't clear out enemies using arrows before entering the room.<br/>
So all said, understand the added risk of choosing stairs 14 and 15 early.

24-27F: Power enemies like Ironhead, Hipadile, and Punisher start showing up, so use Poison Arrows.

33-35F: Ornery Tanks appear, so rush stairs unless you have a way to deal with them.<br/>
One skip option is stairs 11 (It's always night), but be warned that Evil Poofy appears on 33F.<br/>
The other option is stairs 13 (Mostly Mixer monsters), but Mixergon can be quite a threat too.

36F: Low risk if you have Rocks for Cave Mamels, so hunt Grass Poppas to stock up on grass.<br/>
However, VeniScorp spawns, so be careful not to get stung if you don't have an Antidote Grass or Peach.<br/>
Reading an Immunity Scroll helps reduce the risk of accidents if you plan on lingering a long time.

37-38F: MC Wizards appear, so rush stairs unless you have a way to deal with them.<br/>
Similar to Ornery Tanks, one option is to choose stairs 11 (It's always night) to skip past them,<br/>
but Bad Colocolums slain by other monsters can one-shot you by rolling into you if you're unlucky.

40-45F: This floor range is particularly dangerous at night.<br/>
Dark Scarabbit, Vile Pumpanshee, and Sinister Porkon are major threats on 41-43F,<br/>
and while not as bad as that combination, Dark Ornery Tanks appear on 44-45F.<br/>
Dangers during the day include Nashagga (40-42F), Sky Dragons (41-43F), and Nigiri Boss (44-45F).<br/>Some players save stairs 13 (Mostly Mixer monsters) until this final stretch to have an easier time.
