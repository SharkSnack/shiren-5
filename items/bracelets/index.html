Can. Arm Bracelet

Makes projectiles pierce through walls and creatures.
Powerful when used with arrows, Gitan, or talismans.
Items with piercing properties will hit monsters immune to projectiles.
However, be sure to unequip it while not in use to prevent accidents,
especially on floors where Swordsman, Gazer, and Field Knave monsters appear.

Versus Mode:
Equip this bracelet and throw an item toward a wall to send it at the other player.
Anything that can be thrown (arrows, grass, Gitan, etc.) can hit the player,
so it's easily one of the most powerful items to turn the tide in this mode.
Unlike magic bullets from staves, thrown items can be sent even when
you're on different floors, making a thrown Swap Staff very effective.

Mojo Bracelet

Direct attacks or projectiles can deal a critical hit if you're carrying cursed or sealed items.
Items in pots are excluded, and the chance increases with more items.
It limits inventory space, but it's among the strongest bracelets in the game.
Effects can be stacked using 2 bracelet resonance to raise critical hit rate further.
(1 bracelet = 18 items for 100%, 2 bracelets = 9 items for 100%)
Resonates with Violent Blade. (direct attacks occasionally inflict slow)

Formula:
Critical hit rate = 5xy + 10%, x = number of cursed or sealed items, y = number of bracelets

Heal Bracelet

Significantly speeds up hunger rate and HP regeneration rate while it's equipped.
The hunger rate increase makes it tricky to use even with the CR Diet rune,
but the ability to quickly recover from high damage encounters is worth the cost.
Remains in effect while Satiated, but no effect while Starving.

Fantastic in Old Road, where fullness depletes in real time instead of being turn based.
Synthesize a weapon with this bracelet and a Heal Pot to create the Healing rune.

Without CR Diet rune:
1 bracelet = heal 3 HP per turn, fullness depletion of 0, 1, 0, 1, 1 ... (6 fullness / 10 turns)
2 bracelets = heal 6 HP per turn, fullness depletion of 1, 1, 1, 1, 2 ... (12 fullness / 10 turns)

Explosion Bracelet

Randomly makes you explode, halving your current HP like an Explosion Trap.
The number of turns that elapse before activating varies, and isn't tied to specific actions.
Explosion damage can be nullified by a Zen Pot or the Anti-Blast+ shield rune.
Useful for grilling onigiri, or for creating a safe space inside walls to cycle day and night.
Remembering which dungeons it appears in helps avoid accidents in shops.

Enemies tend to walk straight, so an inescapable space can be created in a hallway.
